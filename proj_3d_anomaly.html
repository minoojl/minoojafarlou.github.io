3D Anomaly Detection

This research investigates volumetric deep learning methods for structural anomaly detection in three-dimensional data such as industrial parts and medical scans. The focus is on designing representations that are robust to noise, misalignment, and sparse data regimes.

Technical Summary

The pipeline combines voxelized feature extraction, energy-based anomaly scoring, and uncertainty quantification. Training employs a hybrid objective that blends reconstruction with margin-based penalties, explicitly separating inlier and outlier distributions. This yields improved generalization and interpretable scoring, with implications for automated inspection in industrial and biomedical settings.

Impact

This project contributes to the growing literature on 3D anomaly detection by highlighting the role of robust latent representations in safety-critical domains. Future work will explore extensions to point clouds and mesh domains, as well as real-time deployment in additive manufacturing pipelines.
